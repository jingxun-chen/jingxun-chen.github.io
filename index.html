Zhaoyu Liu

I'm a Masters student in Biomedical Engineering at Johns Hopkins University, where I'm working on fabricating and designing foldable microelectrode arrays for organoids and 3D mapping of cardiac organoids.

Previously, I completed my Bachelor's in Biomedical Engineering at the Chinese University of Hong Kong, where I worked on various projects including EEG signal processing, sweat sensor fabrication, and a Virtual Reality system for stroke rehabilitation.

Ongoing Research Projects
Foldable Microelectrode Arrays for Organoids

Currently working on fabricating and designing foldable microelectrode arrays for organoids at Johns Hopkins University. This project aims to develop innovative tools for studying and manipulating organoid structures.
3D Mapping of Cardiac Organoids

Engaged in research on 3D mapping of cardiac organoids at Johns Hopkins University. This project focuses on developing advanced techniques for visualizing and analyzing the complex structures of cardiac organoids.
